Risk Identification

Source-based risk mapping from the Equifax 2017 breach — all risks traced to documented failures
Part 1
🔓 Technical Vulnerabilities Cyber
Apache Struts CVE-2017-5638 left unpatched for 76 days despite US-CERT alert. Scanning tool failed to detect the exposed component. SSL certificate expired 10 months prior, blinding network monitoring to all suspicious traffic.
R1 — Patch Failure R2 — Monitoring Blind Spot R3 — Lateral Movement
🏛️ Governance Failures Governance
CSO did not report directly to CEO — security lacked executive voice. No independent CRO function existed. Risk ownership was unclear across teams. Cybersecurity was chronically underfunded relative to the company's data custodianship role.
R4 — Structural Accountability Gap R5 — Underfunding
🧠 Cultural Issues Culture
Employees described a "firefighting" culture — reactive rather than proactive. Technical debt accumulated for years without strategic resolution. Risk silos prevented cross-functional awareness. Teams lacked psychological safety to escalate concerns upward.
R6 — Reactive Risk Culture R7 — Technical Debt
📣 Communication Breakdowns Operational
Breach discovered July 29 but not disclosed until September 7 — a 40-day delay. Consumer response directed to a domain resembling a phishing site. Executives sold stock before public disclosure. Public messaging was inconsistent and lacked clarity.
R8 — Disclosure Delay R9 — Reputational Risk R10 — Insider Trading Risk

Risk Treatment Plan

Mitigation strategies, controls, owners, and implementation timeline for top risks
Part 4
MITIGATE — Reduce likelihood/impact TRANSFER — Insurance / contractual shift AVOID — Restructure to eliminate risk ACCEPT — Monitor with residual tolerance
R1 · Cyber · Score 25
Unpatched Critical Vulnerability
MITIGATE
  • Mandate 30-day SLA for all CVSS ≥7.0 vulnerabilities with named owner assigned within 24 hours
  • Implement automated patch management platform (Qualys, Rapid7) with real-time compliance dashboards
  • Require manual verification of all scanner results for critical systems — eliminate single-tool reliance
  • Establish weekly patch review cadence with CISO sign-off on any overdue critical CVEs
  • Conduct quarterly red team exercises to validate patch controls are effective in production
Owner: CISO / IT Ops Timeline: 0–60 days Target Score: ≤8
R2 · Cyber · Score 20
Network Monitoring Failure
MITIGATE
  • Deploy automated SSL/TLS certificate inventory with 30/60/90-day expiry alerts; no manual renewal reliance
  • Implement full SIEM platform with 24/7 SOC monitoring; enable SSL inspection on all internal traffic
  • Establish anomalous egress traffic alerts — flag large or unusual data transfers within 1 hour
  • Require monthly audit of all monitoring tool health and certificate validity chain
  • Set mean-time-to-detect KRI target of ≤24 hours for critical security events
Owner: CISO Timeline: 0–45 days Target Score: ≤6
R3 · Cyber · Score 16
Network Segmentation Failure
MITIGATE
  • Implement Zero Trust Architecture — no implicit trust between network zones; verify every connection
  • Segment all PII-bearing systems into isolated VLANs with strict least-privilege access controls
  • Deploy micro-segmentation for database tier; limit lateral movement radius to single application
  • Conduct annual penetration tests specifically targeting lateral movement scenarios
Owner: IT Architecture Timeline: 60–180 days Target Score: ≤8
R4 · Governance · Score 12
Governance Structure Gap
AVOID
  • Create independent CRO role reporting directly to CEO and Board Risk Committee
  • Elevate CISO to C-suite with direct board access; include in all executive strategy discussions
  • Establish Board Risk Committee with quarterly reporting cadence and mandatory cyber risk agenda item
  • Define and publish RACI for all enterprise risk activities; review annually
Owner: CEO / Board Timeline: 0–60 days Target Score: ≤4
R8 · Operational · Score 12
Disclosure & Incident Response Failure
MITIGATE + AVOID
  • Pre-approve board-level disclosure protocols: breach confirmed → public notification within 72 hours
  • Establish dedicated incident response team with external forensics firm on retainer
  • Conduct annual tabletop breach simulation exercises including executive and board participants
  • Ban stock transactions for all executives from the moment any security incident is declared
Owner: Legal / CEO Timeline: 30–90 days Target Score: ≤4
R5 + R7 · Governance + Culture · Score 8–10
Underfunding & Technical Debt
MITIGATE
  • Benchmark cybersecurity spend vs. financial sector peers; increase budget to ≥6% of IT spend
  • Establish 3-year technical debt reduction roadmap with milestone-based funding tied to risk reduction
  • Link executive compensation to ERM KPIs — security risk metrics included in bonus calculations
  • Shift from annual to continuous risk assessment cycle; integrate risk into strategic planning process
Owner: CFO / CEO Timeline: 0–12 months Target Score: ≤4
